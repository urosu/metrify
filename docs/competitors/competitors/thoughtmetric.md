## Positioning

ThoughtMetric sells itself as "Marketing Attribution for e-commerce" with the tagline "Measure what drives revenue. Scale what works." (homepage). The pitch is multi-touch attribution for sub-mid-market DTC operators who want Triple-Whale-style channel truth without Triple-Whale-style pricing or onboarding. Their explicit competitive frame against Triple Whale, Northbeam, Wicked Reports, Cometly, and Polar Analytics is "simpler setup, easier to use," "faster time to value," and "deeper insights, broader integrations." Pricing is the loud differentiator: $99/mo entry, every feature included at every tier ("No gated features. No premium add-ons. No paying extra to see the metrics that matter."). Unlike Shopify-only peers, ThoughtMetric supports Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Magento — making it one of the few attribution tools available to Woo merchants in this category.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify / WooCommerce / BigCommerce / Magento
- Pulled: orders, revenue, line items / SKUs, customers (new vs. returning), AOV, refunds (implicit from "net profit" computation).
- Computed: Total Sales, Net Profit, AOV, LTV, repeat purchase behavior, retention patterns, geographic revenue distribution, item-level loyalty, "high-frequency pairings" (bundle co-purchase analysis).

### Source: Meta Ads
- Pulled (per integration page): Ad Spend, Impressions, Clicks, CPC, Engagement, Comments, Likes.
- Computed: ROAS, CPA, Total Sales (Meta-attributed), Net Profit, Conversion Rate, Orders, AOV — all computed via ThoughtMetric's first-party pixel + post-purchase survey + Conversion API stack rather than relying on Meta's self-reported numbers.
- Pitch quote: "accurate tracking even with iOS14" through proprietary attribution modeling.

### Source: Google Ads / TikTok / Pinterest / Snapchat / LinkedIn / Bing
- Pulled: spend, impressions, clicks at campaign / ad set / ad level.
- Computed: ROAS, CPA, attributed orders, attributed revenue under whichever of the 5 attribution models is selected.

### Source: Klaviyo / Postscript / Attentive / other email + SMS
- Pulled: email/SMS sends, opens, clicks (precise field list not published).
- Computed: email and SMS contribution to revenue under multi-touch attribution; "Email and SMS performance" is enumerated as a tracked channel.

### Source: First-party pixel + Post-Purchase Survey
- Pixel: server-side tagging "to bypass tracking issues caused by iOS 14 and Ad Blockers."
- Survey: configurable "How did you hear about us?" question with custom response options and full translation support; survey responses feed the multi-touch model — explicit pitch is "first party data for hard-to-track channels such as podcasts or billboards."

### Attribution windows
- Selectable lookback per their marketing attribution page: **7, 14, 30, 60, 90 days**.
- Five attribution models, verbatim from the Intercom help article:
  - **Multi-Touch (default)** — "proprietary data driven attribution model" that splits credit "across all of the marketing channels that influenced a sale," combining pixel + survey signals.
  - **First Touch** — "provide all the credit for any order to the first touchpoint in a conversion path."
  - **Last Touch** — "provide all the credit for any order to the last touchpoint in a conversion path."
  - **Position Based** — "assigns 40% credit to the first touch, 40% credit to the last touch, and splits the remaining 20% across the remaining touchpoints."
  - **Linear Paid** — "gives equal credit to all touchpoints in the customer journey that involve paid media."

### Computed metrics enumerated across feature pages
ROAS, CPA, AOV, LTV, MER, Conversion Rate, Orders, Add-to-carts, Items sold, Total Sales, Net Profit, New vs. Returning customer split, Returning customer sales, Average time to order, Touchpoints to order count.

## Unique strengths

- **Flat pricing across feature set.** "Every feature is included in every plan. No add-ons, or surprise charges." — pricing page. Direct contrast to Triple Whale, Northbeam, and Polar, where attribution-quality features are typically gated to higher tiers.
- **Multi-platform ecommerce coverage.** Shopify + WooCommerce + BigCommerce + Magento native — the only competitor in the comparable price band that supports Woo natively. Strong relevance for the Nexstage ICP.
- **Five attribution models with clear definitions, including a "Linear Paid"-only variant** that excludes organic touchpoints — uncommon in the category and aimed at paid-media decision-makers.
- **Post-purchase survey baked into the attribution model** (not a separate Fairing/KnoCommerce add-on), with custom response options and full translation. The survey signal feeds the default multi-touch model directly.
- **AI Connectors for Claude and ChatGPT.** Rather than building a chat UI in-app, they expose data outward to general-purpose AI assistants — lower R&D cost than competitors building in-house GenBI.
- **Founder-accessible support model.** Multiple verbatim reviews mention founder-level response: "the Founder himself responded very quickly to my questions" (Poseidon Animal Health). ThoughtMetric advertises this explicitly: "Real people who understand attribution and e-commerce. No chatbots, no ticket queues."
- **Soft-cap pricing model.** "If you exceed your plan's pageview limit two months in a row, we'll notify you and work with you to upgrade" — no hard overages, no surprise bills.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **No Amazon ads/seller-central integration** — most-frequently-cited gap across multiple Capterra reviews, dating back to 2021 and not yet shipped per the public integrations roster.
- **Data discrepancies and slow support response when they occur.** The two 1-star reviews (Capterra Dec 2023, Shopify Mar 2026) both centre on tracking accuracy disagreements with no resolution after 10+ days.
- **24-hour data lag** mentioned by at least one reviewer — at odds with the "orders come in instantly" pitch and likely indicates batch refresh on certain dashboards.
- **No GA4 integration listed** despite GA4 being the dominant web-analytics surface for ecommerce. GA4 isn't on the integrations page, only Google Tag Manager.
- **No GSC integration.** Search Console isn't on the integrations roster — organic-search reporting is gated to UTM data flowing through their pixel.
- **No mobile app.** No iOS or Android client referenced anywhere; competitors like Triple Whale and Polar both ship mobile.
- **Small public review footprint.** 13 Shopify App Store reviews, 15 Capterra reviews, no Trustpilot listing surfaced in search. For a 1,200-customer-claimed product this is a thin signal base; conclusions on UX should be weighted accordingly.
- **Limited public UI documentation.** Marketing pages use stylised widget mockups rather than labelled product screenshots; the live app sits behind a login. Detailed UI patterns (table behaviours, drill-down chrome, filter UX) are not observable from public sources.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **Pricing-model template worth studying.** Their flat-feature-set pricing with a soft pageview cap is the cleanest articulation of "no feature gating" we've documented in the category. If Nexstage follows a similar pricing model, ThoughtMetric is the closest pricing-page reference. Their explicit "no surprise bills" overage policy is also reassurance copy worth modelling.
- **Multi-platform coverage is rare and load-bearing.** ThoughtMetric is one of the only multi-touch attribution products that natively supports WooCommerce. For Nexstage's Woo-merchant ICP, ThoughtMetric is likely the most direct apples-to-apples competitor we'll encounter at this price tier.
- **They expose 5 attribution models with explicit definitions.** Our `MetricSourceResolver` thesis is partly about source picking, partly about attribution-window picking; ThoughtMetric's model picker (with the explicit "Linear Paid" variant that excludes organic touchpoints) is a worth-noting interaction pattern. Their windows are 7/14/30/60/90 — a useful default set.
- **Post-purchase survey integrated into the attribution model is a feature differentiator, not a side product.** They use survey signal alongside pixel data to drive their default multi-touch model — that's a direct analog to our 6-source thesis (Real / Store / Facebook / Google / GSC / GA4): treat zero-party signal as a first-class source rather than a nice-to-have. They don't expose a "Real" lens equivalent though — there's no obvious "this is what your store actually saw, raw" view; everything flows through their pixel/model.
- **No GA4 on the integrations page is striking.** They explicitly opt out of relaying GA4 data, betting on their own pixel as the source of truth for web sessions. This is the opposite of Nexstage's "expose all sources side-by-side" approach. Worth noting in source-strategy synthesis.
- **Order Profiles screen is the unique customer-analytics surface to watch.** Their per-order touchpoint timeline (under Customer Analytics > Order Profiles) is the one screen pattern that doesn't have a clear analog in our other competitor profiles. Couldn't get screenshots; flag for follow-up if a video walkthrough surfaces.
- **Founder-led support is a recurring positive theme.** Multiple reviews praise founder-level response time. As Nexstage scales, this is the kind of moat that erodes — but it's a real reason customers choose them today, and worth understanding why support quality keeps appearing alongside attribution accuracy as a primary praise vector.
- **Public UI was not directly observable.** All product pages use stylised mockups; the live app sits behind a paid trial. If detailed UI capture is needed for synthesis, plan for a trial signup or a screen-recorded walkthrough on YouTube as the next research step.
