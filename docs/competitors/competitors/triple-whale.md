## Positioning

Triple Whale sells "the complete intelligence platform for ecommerce" to Shopify-first DTC brands that have outgrown native Shopify reports + a spreadsheet of platform-reported ROAS. They replace Northbeam (attribution), Polar Analytics / Lifetimely (Shopify BI dashboards), Hyros / Wicked Reports (pixel attribution), and the human cost of stitching Meta / Google / TikTok reporting together. The differentiating angle has shifted in 2025–2026 from "Triple Pixel beats iOS-broken Meta attribution" toward an agentic narrative — "Moby Agents" running creative-fatigue, anomaly-detection, pacing, and measurement tasks autonomously on top of an "AI-optimized data warehouse" (Triple Whale Data Platform).

## Pricing & tiers

Pricing is GMV-banded (last-12-months gross merchandise value): Up to $2M, $2M–$3M, $3M–$5M, $5M–$7M, $7M+. The headline tier price quoted at the lowest GMV band differs by source, which itself signals opaque pricing.

| Tier | Price (entry GMV band) | What's included | Common upgrade trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founders Dash (Free Forever) | $0 | Summary board, benchmarks, email reports, web analytics, 1-question post-purchase survey, 2 users, 12-month lookback, mobile app, free integrations to Meta/Google/TikTok/Klaviyo/etc. | Wanting Triple Pixel attribution, Moby AI beyond product guidance, multi-touch attribution, or unlimited lookback |
| Free (basic plan) | $0 | Basic integrations, first/last-click attribution, standard post-purchase survey, Moby Chat (product guidance only), up to 10 users | Same as above |
| Starter | $179–$299/mo (entry GMV band; varies by source/snapshot) | Multi-touch attribution, advanced post-purchase survey, "detailed Summary page", influencer/affiliate analysis, Sonar Send, custom metric builder, ad budget management, unlimited lookback, unlimited users | Wanting subscription data, creative analytics, cohorts, no-code dashboard builder |
| Advanced | $259–$389/mo (entry band) | Adds: subscription data, "total impact" attribution, Creative Analytics, Product Analytics, Cohort Analysis, no-code dashboard builder, segmentation builder, Sonar Optimize, all-LLM access for AI Visibility, multi-store reporting | Need MMM, SQL editor, unlimited custom dashboards |
| Professional / Enterprise | $749/mo (entry band) | Adds: Marketing Mix Modeling (Compass), SQL editor, unlimited custom dashboards | $20M+ GMV; need white-glove + SLAs |
| Custom Enterprise+ | Custom (~$279/mo base referenced; effectively "talk to sales" for $20M+ GMV) | Flexible data warehouse, tailored attribution modelling, SLA-backed guarantees, priority support, QBRs, custom AI tuning, enterprise security | — |

Notes:
- Pricing scales with GMV; conjura.com states a $6M GMV brand sits in the $5M–$7M bracket at "$1,129/month, totaling $11,290 per year with 2 months free" (2025 snapshot).
- Add-ons are billed separately: Conversion Analytics, Retention features, Compass (MMM + incrementality), and "Moby AI Pro" credits ("No auto overages" — credits pause when depleted).
- Annual discount: "Get two months free" with yearly billing; monthly listed prices generally assume 12-month commitment.
- 3rd-party Tekpon listing references three older tier names ("Growth $129 / Pro $199 / Enterprise $279") that don't match the current `triplewhale.com/pricing` page — Triple Whale has clearly re-priced and re-shaped tiers multiple times; this is the basis for "pricing creep" complaints below.

Pricing pages do not surface a unit/seat axis explicitly — paid plans state "unlimited users". The scale axis is GMV, not seats or stores (multi-store roll-up requires Advanced+).

## Integrations

Triple Whale lists "60+ one-click connections" plus a "Data-In API" for arbitrary sources. Grouped from the public integrations page:

- **Ecommerce platforms (data sources):** Shopify (primary), BigCommerce, WooCommerce. Marketplaces: Amazon NA/EU/FE, Walmart.
- **Ad platforms (sources):** Meta, Google Ads, TikTok, Pinterest, Snapchat, Microsoft Ads (Bing), LinkedIn, Reddit, StackAdapt, Taboola, Outbrain, MNTN, Criteo, AppLovin, AdRoll. Sonar Optimize sends server-side conversions back to Meta, Google, TikTok, Reddit, X (April 2026).
- **Email/SMS:** Klaviyo, Yotpo SMS, Attentive, Postscript, Omnisend (the new Email & SMS Attribution Dashboard, April 2026 beta, unifies all four).
- **Subscriptions:** Recharge, Skio.
- **Reviews / surveys / CX:** Yotpo Reviews, Okendo, Fairing, Gorgias.
- **Affiliate:** ShareASale, Awin, Rakuten, Impact.
- **Logistics:** ShipBob, ShipStation.
- **Search / web:** Google Search Console, Google Sheets.
- **Data warehouse / SQL:** BigQuery, Snowflake (push to your own warehouse on Advanced/Enterprise).
- **Comms:** Slack, X (Twitter).
- **Push to ad platforms (destinations):** Meta CAPI, Google Ads, TikTok, Reddit, X via Sonar Optimize. Audience Sync to Microsoft Ads added April 2026. "Attribution Sync for Meta" pipes Triple Whale's attribution model directly into Meta optimisation.

**Notable gaps observed:**
- Magento not supported (cited explicitly by 3rd-party reviews as a dealbreaker for non-Shopify merchants).
- No mention of Bing Webmaster, no GA4 (their analytics narrative explicitly competes with GA4 rather than ingesting it).
- No native TikTok Shop / Instagram Shop sales-side integration listed (only TikTok Ads).
- "GA4" is conspicuously absent from the integrations list — Triple Whale's pitch is to *replace* GA4, not enrich it.

## Product surfaces (their app's information architecture)

This is a partial inventory built from public marketing + KB references. (KB pages 403'd to WebFetch; what follows is derived from blog walkthroughs, 3rd-party tutorials, and the public marketing pages.)

- **Summary Dashboard** — "What is the state of my business right now?" Default lens; blended KPIs across store + ad platforms.
- **Pixel / Attribution dashboard** — "Which channels and creatives actually drove revenue?" Multi-touch / first-click / last-click / "Total Impact" attribution comparisons.
- **Pixel Events Manager** (new April 2026) — "What is my pixel actually firing?" Real-time event log, hourly breakdowns, device/browser segmentation.
- **Creative Analysis Dashboard** ("Creative Cockpit") — "Which creatives are working / fatiguing?" Cross-platform creative performance.
- **Product Analytics** — "Which SKUs / categories are driving growth?" SKU-level performance + journey mapping.
- **Cohort Analysis / CLTV** — "What does retention look like by cohort?" Segmentable by first product purchased, discount code, channel, etc.
- **Benchmarks (formerly "Trends")** — "How do my CPA/CPC/CPM/ROAS compare to industry peers?" Anonymised peer benchmarks from 11k–20k+ Triple Whale customers.
- **Lighthouse** — "What anomalies should I act on?" Anomaly detection on spend / orders / inventory + AI Audiences + Rules Report.
- **Compass** — "What's the true incremental effect of each channel?" Unified MMM + MTA + incrementality testing layer.
- **Marketing Mix Modeling** — Sub-surface within Compass (Pro tier and up).
- **Incrementality Testing** — Conversion-lift experiments via randomized controlled trials (Meta first, expanding).
- **Email & SMS Attribution Dashboard** (April 2026 beta) — Unified Klaviyo / Attentive / Omnisend / Postscript revenue + engagement.
- **Web Analytics** — Custom events, path/funnel analysis, site search, page speed (expanded April 2 launch).
- **Sonar Send dashboard** — Email/SMS flow performance enrichment view.
- **Sonar Optimize** — Server-side data enrichment to Meta/Google/TikTok/Reddit/X (configuration surface).
- **Custom Dashboard Builder** — No-code dashboard construction (Advanced+); SQL editor on Pro+.
- **Segmentation Builder** — Custom data segments (Advanced+).
- **Custom Metric Builder** — Composite metrics on Summary (Starter+).
- **Ad Budget Management / Ad Platform Controls** — Direct budget/bid/status edits to Meta, Google, Pinterest, Snap, TikTok from inside Triple Whale (April 2 launch).
- **Post-Purchase Survey** — Standard (Free) → Advanced (Starter+) survey builder for self-reported attribution.
- **Audience Sync** — RFM and behavioural audiences pushed back to ad platforms (Meta first; Microsoft Ads added April 2026).
- **Moby Chat** — Conversational query of your data, in-app. Sub-surface inside every dashboard.
- **Moby Agents** — Autonomous agents: Media Buying Agent, Retention Marketing Agent, Website Performance Agent, Anomaly Detection Agent, Measurement Agent, Creative Strategy Agent, Order & Revenue Pacing Agent, Revenue Anomaly Agent.
- **Moby 2** (rolling out April 2026) — AI rebuild adding ad-creative generation + direct Meta publishing, weekly reports, automated campaign optimization.
- **Settings / Integrations / Data-In API / Pixel installer** — connection surfaces; the new "Automated Pixel Installation" CLI auto-injects pixel code for headless/custom stores.

T1 competitor — that's ~25+ distinct surfaces (consistent with the "75+ pre-built dashboards" claim, which presumably are templates inside Self-Serve Analytics rather than top-level nav items).

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify (and Woo / BigCommerce)
- Pulled: orders, line items, customers, products, refunds, discounts, subscriptions (via Recharge/Skio), shipping/fulfilment status (via ShipBob/ShipStation linkage).
- Computed: revenue, AOV, MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio), ncROAS (new-customer ROAS), POAS (profit on ad spend), nCAC (new-customer acquisition cost), CLTV by cohort (60/90 LTVs cited on mobile-app marketing), gross profit / net profit (when COGS provided), repeat rate.
- Attribution windows: stated "look back up to 60 days, with 180 days planned" (older blog reference); on paid plans Triple Pixel offers "unlimited lookback".

### Source: Meta Ads
- Pulled: campaign / ad set / ad spend, impressions, clicks, platform-reported conversions, ROAS, CPM, CPC.
- Computed: Triple-Pixel-attributed revenue side-by-side with Meta-reported revenue; "Total Impact" attribution model; first/last-click splits; nc-ROAS, blended-ROAS.
- Attribution windows: configurable (default 1d-click / 7d-click families exposed; full lookback per pixel data on paid tiers).
- Push back: Sonar Optimize sends enriched server-side conversions via Meta CAPI; Attribution Sync for Meta pipes Triple Whale's MTA model into Meta's optimization engine.

### Source: Google Ads
- Pulled: campaign / ad group / ad spend, impressions, clicks, conversions, ROAS, search-term performance.
- Computed: Pixel-attributed revenue alongside Google-reported; multi-touch credit allocation across Google + others.
- Push back: Sonar Optimize for Google Ads (server-side conversions / enhanced conversions enrichment).

### Source: TikTok / Pinterest / Snapchat / Reddit / X / Microsoft Ads / LinkedIn / AppLovin / etc.
- Pulled: spend / impressions / clicks / platform-reported conversions per platform.
- Computed: blended view across all platforms in Summary's "Blended" lens; included in Benchmarks comparisons.
- Push back: Sonar Optimize for TikTok, Reddit, X (April 2026); Audience Sync to Microsoft Ads (April 2026).

### Source: Klaviyo / Attentive / Postscript / Omnisend
- Pulled: flow + campaign engagement and revenue; UTM-tagged events.
- Computed: Sonar Send revenue enrichment; new unified Email & SMS Attribution Dashboard (April 2026).
- Marketing claim: "22% Increase in Klaviyo Flow Revenue" with Sonar Send.

### Source: Triple Pixel (their own first-party pixel)
- Pulled: first-party events (page view, add-to-cart, purchase, custom events), session/device fingerprint, ad-click params from Meta/Google/Snap/Pinterest/TikTok, full customer journey.
- Computed: identity graph stitching device → person; multi-touch attribution; "Total Impact" model; deduplicated server-side events to Meta CAPI / Google / TikTok / Reddit / X.
- Marketing claims (verbatim from public pages): "captures data that other platforms miss"; "end-to-end customer journey tracking"; setup "in less than 10 minutes". Notably Triple Whale's *current* public marketing on `triplewhale.com/pixel` does **not** publish a numeric iOS-reclaim percentage despite earlier-era marketing implying one (older 3rd-party copy referenced "75% of users" affected by ATT, but that's the iOS-loss number, not Triple Whale's reclaim number).

### Source: Google Search Console
- Pulled: queries, impressions, clicks, position. Listed under "Data & Analytics" integration group; depth of GSC dashboarding not visible publicly.

### Source: Amazon / Walmart
- Pulled: marketplace sales (NA/EU/FE for Amazon).

### Source: Stripe / BigQuery / Snowflake
- Stripe pulled for subscription / payment data. BigQuery and Snowflake are bidirectional — Triple Whale can pipe its data to your warehouse on Advanced+.

## Key UI patterns observed

KB articles 403 to WebFetch in this environment, so several screen-level UI descriptions are reconstructed from public blog walkthroughs, the homepage, and 3rd-party reviews. Where I could not directly observe a screen, I say so explicitly.

### Summary Dashboard

- **Path/location:** Default landing page. Top-level nav item, sidebar.
- **Layout (prose):** Top date-range and store-switcher controls (period-comparison toggle implied by "vs prior period" delta language). Body is organized as **collapsible sections by data integration** — by default sections include Pinned, Store Metrics, Meta, Google, Klaviyo, Web Analytics (Triple Pixel), and Custom Expenses. Each section is a grid of **draggable metric tiles**. A "table view" toggle pivots the same tile grid into a dense single-table layout. The hero screenshot on the homepage is labelled "Summary Dashboard Blended Metrics" and shows a row of KPI tiles across the top of the canvas.
- **UI elements (concrete):** KPI tile shows headline value + period-vs-period delta. Hovering a tile reveals a 📌 pin icon (the help-center copy literally references the pushpin emoji). "Edit Dashboard" button enters drag-edit mode and surfaces "Create Custom Metric". Sections can be hidden. New (April 2026) **on-demand refresh button** pulls the latest data from connected integrations with a real-time status cycling display ("Refreshing Meta…", etc.).
- **Interactions:** Drag-and-drop reordering of tiles; pin to "Pinned" section; pivot to table view; click metric to drill into detail; Moby Chat sidebar available on every dashboard for natural-language query. Filters can segment Shopify and Ad data.
- **Metrics shown (typical defaults referenced across reviews and blog):** Revenue, Net Profit, ROAS, MER, ncROAS, POAS, nCAC, CAC, AOV, LTV (60/90), Total Ad Spend, Sessions, Conversion Rate, Refund Rate, plus per-platform spend/ROAS sub-tiles. Mobile app surfaces "real-time MER, ncROAS & POAS".
- **Source/screenshot:** Homepage hero (`triplewhale.com`) shows the Blended Metrics view; `triplewhale.com/blog/build-the-perfect-data-analytics-dashboard` includes a Summary screenshot (not described in the text fetched). KB page `kb.triplewhale.com/en/articles/5725275-track-kpis-with-the-summary-dashboard` is the canonical guide (403 to WebFetch).

### Attribution / Pixel Dashboard

- **Path/location:** Top-level nav.
- **Layout (prose):** Channel breakdown table with side-by-side columns for Triple Pixel attribution vs platform-reported metrics vs first/last-click. "Total Impact" model is selectable as an attribution lens (Advanced+). On-demand refresh button mirrors the one on Summary (April 2026).
- **UI elements:** Attribution-model selector (dropdown), date range, channel grouping. Specific column / row count not directly observable from public pages.
- **Interactions:** Switching the attribution lens reflows the channel revenue numbers.
- **Source/screenshot:** UI details not available beyond marketing-page descriptions.

### Benchmarks (formerly "Trends")

- **Path/location:** Sidebar > Benchmarks.
- **Layout (prose):** Compares your metrics to anonymised peer averages across "20,000+ Triple Whale customers" (homepage figure; Trends launch blog cited 11,000+). Four KPIs surfaced as primary: CPA, CPC, CPM, ROAS. Platform tabs: Meta, TikTok, Google, Bing, Twitter, Amazon, Pinterest, Snap, plus a "Blended" view. Filter rail offers Industry vertical, Annual revenue range, AOV band, Time period (last 7 / 30 days). Visual is described as a "graph comparing the benchmark data to your data" — plausibly a line + benchmark band, but the text doesn't describe chart type concretely.
- **UI elements:** Industry vertical filter, revenue-band filter, AOV-band filter, time toggle (7d/30d), platform tabs.
- **Interactions:** Filter to your peer cohort; visualize delta between your metric and the peer benchmark.
- **Metrics shown:** CPA, CPC, CPM, ROAS per platform.
- **Source/screenshot:** `triplewhale.com/blog/trends-benchmarking`.

### Lighthouse (anomaly detection + AI audiences)

- **Path/location:** Sidebar > Lighthouse (or surfaced as alerts in-app + push notifications on mobile).
- **Layout (prose):** Anomaly inbox / alert list — entries fire on suspicious variances in ad spend, inventory, and order data ("Orders Anomalie", "Spend Anomalie" terminology in their own copy). Includes AI Audiences sub-section that auto-builds RFM segments and pushes them to Meta. Rules Report monitors automated rules.
- **UI elements:** Alert cards with severity / metric / suggested action. Pre-built RFM audience tiles ("6 already built segments"). Rules dashboard.
- **Interactions:** Acknowledge alert, drill into anomaly cause, push audience to Meta.
- **Source/screenshot:** `triplewhale.com/lighthouse` redirects to Moby AI marketing page (Triple Whale appears to have folded Lighthouse messaging into Moby + Anomaly Detection Agent in 2025–2026). UI details not directly verified beyond marketing copy.

### Moby Chat

- **Path/location:** Persistent right-rail / floating button on every dashboard.
- **Layout (prose):** Conversational chat panel — natural-language questions ("which campaign drove the most new customers last week?"), returns generated charts, tables, forecasts. Supports natural language *or* SQL.
- **UI elements:** Chat thread; embedded charts/tables in responses; "ask in natural language or SQL" toggle.
- **Interactions:** Type query, receive answer + visualization; export to dashboard or email; multi-turn chat.
- **Source/screenshot:** `triplewhale.com/moby-ai`.

### Moby Agents

- **Path/location:** Sidebar > Moby > Agents (or similar; April 2 launch).

Each agent runs autonomously and writes outputs back to dashboards or email.
- **UI elements:** Per-agent configuration card; output destination selector (dashboard vs email vs Slack).
- **Pricing:** Credit-based ("Moby AI Pro") with "no auto overages" — credits pause when depleted (a notable choice — fail-closed billing).
- **Source/screenshot:** `triplewhale.com/blog/product-event` and `triplewhale.com/moby-ai`.

### Compass (MMM + MTA + Incrementality)

- **Path/location:** Pro tier feature; sidebar Compass.

- **UI details:** Not directly observable from public pages (gated behind Pro tier; product page redirected/404'd in fetch).

## Unique strengths

- **Triple Pixel + Sonar = closed-loop attribution-then-optimization.** Few competitors both *measure* via a first-party pixel and *push* enriched server-side conversions back to ad platforms with deduplication against the platform's own pixel. Sonar Optimize claims "17% avg. increase in ROAS in first 30 days" and "22% Increase in Klaviyo Flow Revenue" (Sonar Send). These numbers appear on `triplewhale.com/sonar` as marketing claims.
- **Moby Agents are the most aggressive agentic-AI rollout in DTC analytics as of April 2026.** 8 named agents, credit-based pricing with fail-closed billing ("no auto overages"), and a Measurement Agent that automatically reconciles MMM vs MTA vs platform-reported. No comparable competitor surface as of this research date.
- **Native mobile app is genuinely a differentiator.** Push notifications on revenue milestones, home-screen widgets, full feature parity with desktop on the free plan; multiple reviews call out that it surpasses Northbeam / Lifetimely / Polar mobile.
- **Benchmarks dashboard with 11k–20k+ peer dataset.** Industry-cohort comparison filtered by vertical, revenue band, AOV band — that aggregated dataset is a network-effect moat smaller competitors cannot match.
- **Founders Dash free-forever tier** is unusually generous (12-month lookback, 10 users, mobile, benchmarks, web analytics) and serves as a Trojan horse into paid plans.
- **AI-optimized data warehouse (Triple Whale Data Platform)** with raw SQL access on Pro+ — gives them an Enterprise story (BigQuery / Snowflake bidirectional, "11 trillion rows daily") that pure-play attribution tools lack.
- **Direct ad-platform controls in-app** (budget/bid/status edits to Meta, Google, Pinterest, Snap, TikTok) — closes the loop from "see the metric" to "act on the metric" without leaving the dashboard.
- **Attribution Sync for Meta** — pipes Triple Whale's MTA model directly into Meta's optimization engine. Inverts the usual analytics-tool-as-read-only assumption.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **GMV-based pricing creep is the #1 recurring criticism in 2025–2026 reviews.** Brands report $1,100+/month at $6M GMV; reviewers call out that growth itself triggers price jumps. Multiple sources reference different price points for the same Starter tier ($179 vs $299) — pricing has been re-shaped multiple times, opaquely.
- **Customer support quality is inconsistent and degrades visibly at smaller-account tiers.** Multiple G2 / App Store reviewers report waiting weeks/months for issue resolution despite $600+ monthly bills.
- **Bugs in Moby and the wider platform.** Multiple reviewers report Moby crashing "more than half the time" (older critical reviews) and ongoing bug reports in 2025–2026 (BioPower Pet, April 2026).
- **Attribution accuracy is contested.** Reviewers note discrepancies between Triple Whale's pixel and other measurement tools; AI Systems Commerce specifically observes attribution "feels closer to platform self-reporting than fully independent models" — unflattering given the entire product positioning.
- **Shopify-first; non-Shopify is a second-class citizen.** Despite WooCommerce and BigCommerce being listed as integrations, every comparative review treats Triple Whale as Shopify-only. No Magento, no native TikTok Shop, GA4 absent.
- **Learning curve for attribution.** Pixel needs 5–7 days (or 2–3 days for $4M+ stores) to stabilize; reviewers warn first 2–3 weeks of data is unreliable.
- **UI changes frequently; docs lag.** Direct quote from 2025–2026 reviewer ("UI changes frequently and documentation sometimes lags behind"). Combined with the mid-2025 Lighthouse → Moby Anomaly Detection Agent rebrand and the April 2026 Moby 2 launch, the IA is in flux.
- **Pricing positions out the very SMB segment Triple Whale's free tier attracts.** Head West Guide explicitly says the first-party pixel "won't really be useful" for sub-$10M brands, single-channel brands, or impulse-purchase categories — i.e. the people most likely to be on the Founders Dash free tier are the ones the paid product least helps.
- **Triple Whale's current public Pixel marketing dropped specific reclaim numbers** that earlier-generation marketing implied. It now relies on qualitative language ("captures data others miss"). Older 3rd-party copy referenced the iOS-loss "75% of users" stat; that's the *problem* number, not their *solution* number.
