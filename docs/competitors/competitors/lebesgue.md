## Positioning

Lebesgue brands itself as "The AI CMO for eCommerce Growth," with the homepage tagline "Meet Henri: Your Smartest Marketer, Built Into a Single Prompt." It explicitly bundles four product categories that are typically separate purchases — first-party attribution (Le Pixel), customer LTV / cohort analytics, marketing-mix modeling, and competitor advertising intelligence — under a conversational AI layer. Compared to Triple Whale (their primary cited competitor), Lebesgue's pitch is "around 90% of the functionality...at roughly 50% of the cost," emphasizing a flat $79/mo Analytics tier vs. Triple Whale's GMV-scaled pricing. Croatian-founded (Zagreb, 2019/2020 by Josip Begić), now US-headquartered; raised a $3M seed in January 2025 led by Fil Rouge Capital.

## Pricing & tiers

| Tier | Price | What's included | Common upgrade trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Analytics: Free | $0/mo | Full order history, order & revenue tracking, COGS & profit, ad-channel integrations, weekly & daily reports | LTV/cohort needs, custom costs |
| Analytics: Advanced | $59/mo | + LTV & cohort analysis, in-depth ad-channel analytics, product intelligence, stock-inventory tracking, custom costs | Want competitor tracking or AI Next Steps |
| Analytics: Ultimate | $79/mo | + Competitor tracking, "AI powered Next Steps," "AI powered Creative Strategy," "Lebesgue Delta" | (Top tier on Analytics) |
| Le Pixel: Attribution | $99–$1,499/mo (revenue-scaled) | 1st-party pixel, basic & advanced attribution models, unlimited retention, AI cross-device matching, complete customer journeys, channel/campaign/ad-level insights, first-time vs. repeat split, subscription data | Need server-side / CAPI |
| Le Pixel: Enrichment | $149–$1,649/mo (revenue-scaled) | Everything in Attribution + server-side tracking + Facebook CAPI integration | (Top tier on attribution) |

Le Pixel revenue-band pricing (verbatim from pricing page): $250K–$500K → $99–$149; $500K–$1M → $149–$199; $1M–$1.5M → $219–$269; $1.5M–$2.5M → $249–$324; $2.5M–$5M → $289–$364; $5M–$10M → $499–$599; $10M+ tiers reach $1,499–$1,649. Le Pixel requires an Advanced or Ultimate Analytics plan.

The Shopify App Store listing additionally shows an **Advanced-Small** tier at $19/mo (LTV/cohorts/retention/stock for stores with sub-50 monthly orders) — this tier is not visible on the website pricing page. Annual subscriptions discount 17%.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify / WooCommerce
- Pulled: orders, line items, customers, products, refunds, inventory/stock, subscription data
- Computed: revenue, first-time vs. repeat revenue, AOV, COGS-adjusted profit, predicted LTV, cohort retention by week/month/year, stock-vs-ad-perf coupling, "Lebesgue Delta" (term not defined publicly)
- Custom costs: COGS + custom costs configurable on Advanced tier and up

### Source: Meta Ads
- Pulled: campaign / adset / ad spend, impressions, clicks, conversions, ROAS, ad creative assets, publishing history
- Computed: pixel-attributed revenue (via Le Pixel), benchmarked performance vs. Lebesgue's network, "AI powered Creative Strategy" recommendations, 60-day forecast
- Attribution windows: Multiple models offered through Le Pixel (Shapley Value, Markov Chain, First-Click, Linear, Custom)

### Source: Google Ads
- Pulled: campaign-level spend, clicks, conversions
- Computed: CTR / CPC / CVR benchmarked against Lebesgue's brand network, audit findings against ~50 rule tests
- Attribution windows: Same Le Pixel models as Meta

### Source: TikTok Ads, Microsoft Ads, Pinterest, Amazon
- Pulled: spend + standard performance metrics
- Computed: Roll-up into Business Report; included in Le Pixel attribution; included in MMM

### Source: Klaviyo
- Pulled: campaign performance and email tactics
- Computed: Email-spike-to-repeat-purchase correlation analysis (Henri sample prompt)

### Source: GA4
- Pulled: standard sessions / pageviews / conversions
- Computed: Lebesgue runs audits against GA4 tracking config; Le Pixel is positioned explicitly *vs.* GA4 (a feature page compares "sessions, timing, and customer touchpoints")

### Source: Le Pixel (first-party)
- Pulled: page views, add-to-cart, conversion events, server-side events (Enrichment tier)
- Computed: AI cross-device matching, complete customer journey per order, channel/campaign/ad-level attributed revenue, first-time-vs-repeat by campaign, subscription attribution
- Attribution windows: Shapley Value, Markov Chain, First-Click, Linear, Custom (per Le Pixel page)

### Source: Competitor Intelligence
- Pulled: competitors' active and inactive ads (Meta + Google), email campaign content, estimated ad spend, trending keywords; benchmarks against "20,000+" (homepage) / "25,000+" (vs-Triple-Whale article) brands
- Computed: AI-predicted competitor ad performance, engagement estimates

## Key UI patterns observed

UI details below are from public marketing screenshots / feature-page descriptions only. A logged-in product walkthrough was not accessible.

### Business Report
- **Path/location:** Primary dashboard surface
- **Layout (prose):** Per the Shopify-reporting-app feature page, user picks "the metrics and time period you'd like to analyze," then the system auto-generates a custom report. Layout includes metric-selection dropdowns, a date-range picker, and line/bar charts. Grouping options for day/week/month sit alongside the period selector.
- **UI elements (concrete):** "Color-coded performance indicators (blue for improvements, red for declines)" per the feature page — note this is **blue** for positive, not green, which is unusual. Metric-selection dropdowns. Date range pickers. Line/bar chart canvases.
- **Interactions:** Pick metric → pick range → auto-generate. Custom-report download. Group toggle (day/week/month).
- **Metrics shown:** Revenue, First-time Revenue, Ad Spend (Meta/Google/TikTok/Amazon/Klaviyo), COGS, Profit, ROAS
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/product-features/shopify-reporting-app

### Compare Metrics
- **Path/location:** Reporting section
- **Layout (prose):** Trend tool. User selects a metric, date range, and aggregation level (daily/weekly/monthly). Output shows correlation patterns between metrics (their example: "Facebook purchases and first-time orders").
- **UI elements:** Single-metric trend chart, time-aggregation toggle.
- **Interactions:** Metric selector, range picker, aggregation toggle.
- **Metrics shown:** Any of the metrics in Business Report, plotted over time.
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/product-features/shopify-reporting-app

### Business Overview Table
- **Path/location:** Reporting section
- **Layout (prose):** Tabular view of all KPIs side-by-side for "quick performance assessment."
- **UI elements:** Tabular grid; export/download button.
- **Interactions:** Download for external analysis.
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/product-features/shopify-reporting-app

### LTV & Cohort dashboard
- **Path/location:** Customer/LTV section
- **Layout (prose):** Dashboard displays "average lifetime value metrics, retention rate indicators, user cohorts, channel-specific filtering options" plus revenue/cost/profitability breakdowns.
- **UI elements:** Cohort grid grouped by week/month/year; channel filter; predicted LTV value cards; CAC vs LTV comparison block.
- **Interactions:** Filter by marketing channel (Google/Meta/TikTok), product, geography (state/country).
- **Metrics shown:** Average LTV, predicted LTV, retention rate by cohort, CAC vs LTV, high-LTV products.
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/customer-lifetime-value

### Le Pixel customer-journey view
- **Path/location:** Attribution section, drilldown from an order
- **Layout (prose):** Per the Le Pixel page screenshot description: "a detailed customer journey view and order information, including touchpoints like page views, add to cart, and conversion value."
- **UI elements:** Per-order touchpoint timeline.
- **Metrics shown:** Page views, add-to-cart, conversion value, channel/campaign/ad attribution per touchpoint, first-time vs. repeat flag, subscription flag.
- **Interactions:** Switch attribution model (Shapley / Markov / First-Click / Linear / Custom).
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/le-pixel

### Henri AI chat
- **Path/location:** Sidebar / dedicated AI surface — homepage shows a single-prompt mockup
- **Layout (prose):** Chat-style text-input area; Henri "thinks" while it processes; responses include inline charts and time-based breakdowns alongside explanatory text. Responses include "key takeaways sections, and recommendations formatted as actionable next steps beneath performance analysis charts."
- **UI elements:** Text-input prompt box; inline chart embeds within chat responses; Key Takeaways and Recommendations sub-blocks.
- **Sample prompts (verbatim):**
  - *"Analyze how our store performed over the last 30 days compared to the same period last year."*
  - *"Summarize our Q3 performance versus Q2."*
  - *"Review our Facebook ad creatives from the past 14 days."*
  - *"Analyze the correlation between email-campaign spikes and repeat-purchase revenue."*
- **Source:** https://lebesgue.io/product-features/henri-ai-agent-for-growth

### AI Agents hub

## Unique strengths

- **Bundling of attribution + LTV + MMM + competitor benchmarks under one AI agent** — most peers (Triple Whale, Northbeam, Hyros) sell only attribution; Lebesgue's pitch is "around 90% of the functionality...at roughly 50% of the cost" of Triple Whale.
- **Five attribution models exposed to the user** (Shapley Value, Markov Chain, First-Click, Linear, Custom) — explicit user-facing model switcher per Le Pixel docs; many competitors hide this choice or expose only one.
- **Competitor advertising intelligence** — tracks competitors' Meta + Google ads, email content, estimated ad spend, trending keywords — claimed to be benchmarked against 20,000–25,000 brands. Triple Whale article explicitly calls this out as Triple Whale-absent.
- **Tiered specialized AI agents** with named personas (Henri, Thinking ICP, Creative Frida, Revenue Drop Investigator, Landing Page Strategist, Breakthrough Ads Architect, Headline Hook Generator, Reddit Ads Pathfinder, AI Visibility Master) — distinguished from peers' single chat interface.
- **Aggressive flat-rate pricing on Analytics** ($79/mo Ultimate cap on Analytics) decouples core dashboard from revenue scale; Le Pixel is the only revenue-scaled component.
- **Customer support is the single most-cited praise pattern** across G2, Capterra, Shopify reviews — multiple reviewers name individual team members (Toni, Tea, Josip).
- **EU/Croatia origin** — likely culturally GDPR-fluent; first-party server-side tracking is privacy-positioned.
- **WooCommerce parity is a real differentiator** — most peers (Triple Whale, Northbeam, Polar) are Shopify-only or Shopify-first.
- **Free tier is genuinely useful** for COGS-aware reporting (multiple reviewers run on free).

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **AI insights described as shallow** — Capterra synthesis surfaces complaints like "insights" being "simply noting that CAC increased and conversion rate dropped off."
- **No country-level or geographic breakdown** within a single account (Tomás Manuel J., Capterra).
- **Limited customization / no white-labeling** (Simon C., Robin T., Capterra).
- **Performance issues on large catalogs** (Michal B. cites slow load with 80k SKUs).
- **Free tier feature-gating** — multiple reviewers note real value sits behind paid.
- **Le Pixel revenue-scaling reaches $1,649/mo** at the upper tier — flat-pricing claim only holds for Analytics, not the attribution module brands actually need.
- **No GSC, no Snapchat / LinkedIn / Reddit-as-Ad-Source-direct integration** observed in pricing/integration lists.
- **Historical reliability concerns** — 2022 AppSumo reviewer documented broken links, non-loading pages, and missing onboarding; founder acknowledged surge-driven issues but the review pattern shows the product had quality wobbles during its growth phase.
- **No data freshness claim made publicly** — unusual for an analytics product; one reviewer mentions "occasional delay in data updates."
- **Refunds are case-by-case** (per their help center), with no published refund SLA.

## Notes for Nexstage

Open observations for downstream feature research:

- **Henri's response format is a direct analog to a "natural-language insight" feature** — text + inline charts + Key Takeaways block + Recommendations block. If Nexstage builds an AI explainer, the formatting blueprint is well-validated here. Sample prompts on their feature page give a reusable taxonomy of intents (period-over-period, creative review, channel comparison, email-revenue correlation).
- **They expose the attribution model selector to end users** (Shapley / Markov / First-Click / Linear / Custom). Worth deciding if Nexstage's `MetricSourceResolver` strategy (we pick the source) is positioned vs. user-pickable models — they go the opposite direction and turn it into a UX feature.
- **"Color-coded performance indicators (blue for improvements, red for declines)"** — they use **blue, not green**, for positive deltas. This is unusual and worth noting against our 6-source color tokens (which include `--color-source-google` and `--color-source-facebook` blues). Their choice may be deliberate to avoid R/G colorblindness issues; it conflicts with our existing source-blue semantics.
- **MMM is delivered as a weekly PDF**, not as a live in-app surface — implies real MMM is computationally expensive enough that even a heavily-resourced competitor can't refresh it on demand. They also gate it behind ≥$5K/mo ad spend and ≥3 months history. Useful boundary for our own MMM ambitions.
- **Le Pixel is a paid add-on tied to Analytics tier** — they monetize first-party tracking separately from dashboards, with revenue-banded pricing reaching $1,649/mo. Pattern worth considering for a `Real` source pricing strategy.
- **"AI agents" branding has been refreshed** — older marketing pages list Balancer/Guardian/Spotlight/Echo/Pulse/Strategist/Sentinel/Prophet/Auditor/Sentry; newer page lists Henri/Thinking ICP/Creative Frida/Revenue Drop Investigator/Landing Page Strategist/Breakthrough Ads Architect/Headline Hook Generator/Reddit Ads Pathfinder/AI Visibility Master. They are iterating naming aggressively — agents are a moving target, not a stable product surface.
- **Competitor benchmarks are treated as a network effect** — they advertise the size of their benchmark cohort (20K → 25K brands across two pages, suggesting growth) as a moat. We don't have this data and probably won't soon.
- **No GSC** — direct gap vs. Nexstage's 6-source thesis. They don't have GSC at all, which is a meaningful differentiation lane.
- **Geographic/country drill-down is a known gap** in Lebesgue (cited by reviewer). Could be Nexstage advantage if we expose this clearly per ad account.
- **WooCommerce parity is real** for them — when researching Woo-specific UX, they're a credible reference point (most peers are Shopify-only).
- **They don't publicly publish data-freshness SLAs.** Worth cross-checking how Triple Whale, Polar, etc. position freshness — could be a Nexstage transparency angle.
- **Pricing display gap:** Shopify App Store lists an Advanced-Small at $19/mo not present on the website pricing page — they segment Shopify-app discovery from website pricing.
- **UTM/channel-mapping reference:** their channel taxonomy (Google/Meta/TikTok/Microsoft/Pinterest/Klaviyo/Amazon/email/organic/ChatGPT) is broader than ours and includes ChatGPT as a channel — worth noting against our `ChannelMappingsSeeder.php`.
