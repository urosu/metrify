## Positioning

Hyros sells itself as the heaviest-duty attribution layer on the market for businesses where the path-to-purchase doesn't end at a Shopify checkout — coaching/info products, high-ticket DTC, SaaS trials, and any funnel that involves sales calls. The home page tagline is "Tracking proven to increase AD ROI for the most complex attribution challenges" with a claim of "100% certainty." Public-facing testimonials lead with Tony Robbins ("Allowed us to scale our ad spend by 43%"). Hyros explicitly competes against Triple Whale, Northbeam, Cometly, and AdBeacon, but differentiates on long-window attribution (calls, webinars, weeks-to-close) rather than Shopify-native attribution. The product is invitation-gated (demo required) and is widely described in third-party reviews as the choice once a brand outgrows Triple Whale on ad spend or has a funnel that ends with a phone call.

## Integrations

**Sources (data they pull in):**
- E-commerce / checkout: Shopify (1-click), WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, Stripe, PayPal, ThriveCart, ClickFunnels (v1 + v2), Kajabi, Funnelish, Calendly
- Ads: Meta/Facebook Ads, Google Ads, TikTok Ads (via Conversions API for each)
- Call: CallRail integration (sits "on top of" CallRail), native Hyros DNI (dynamic number insertion) using Twilio pool
- Site instrumentation: Universal tracking script, Google Tag Manager, server-side tagging
- Custom: Full REST API + Zapier

**Destinations (data they push):**
- Conversions API back to Meta, Google, TikTok (the "AI training" loop — feeding deduped, attributed conversions to platform optimisers is the core sell)

**Coverage gaps observed:**
- **No GSC integration** — organic search keyword/query data is not pulled.
- **No GA4 integration** — Hyros positions itself as a *replacement* for GA4 rather than a complement.
- **Klaviyo:** not surfaced as a first-class integration in their public docs index (unlike Triple Whale).
- **Amazon, Walmart, Pinterest Ads:** not advertised.
- **Historical data:** SegMetrics' comparison page calls out that Hyros tracking "starts from installation day" — no retroactive import of past orders/clicks.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify / WooCommerce / checkout systems
- Pulled: orders, line items, customers, refunds, subscription events (renewals, cancellations, upgrades, downgrades), Stripe events
- Computed: attributed revenue per ad/source, LTV per cohort, AOV, refund-adjusted ROAS
- Attribution windows: configurable; long-window support is a core selling point — capable of attributing a sale "from first ad click to the 1000th" touch, with users reporting multi-week and multi-month windows in coaching funnels

### Source: Meta Ads
- Pulled: campaign/adset/ad spend, impressions, clicks; pushed: deduped conversions back via CAPI ("AI Pixel Training")
- Computed: Hyros-attributed conversions and revenue; "No Source" sales bucket; reporting gap vs platform (Hyros claims **29–33% more sales captured** than native Ads Manager)

### Source: Google Ads
- Pulled/pushed: same model as Meta — clicks/cost in, conversions out via Google Ads enhanced conversions
- Notable: matches Google Search clicks to booked calls and closed deals via Twilio DNI

### Source: TikTok Ads
- Pulled: spend/clicks; pushed: server-side conversions via TikTok Events API

### Source: Phone calls (Twilio DNI + CallRail)
- Pulled: inbound call sessions, caller-id, call duration, outcome tags from CRM/phone system (qualified, booked, sold)
- Computed: call-attributed sales, call quality scoring, close rate by rep, attribution from call back to original ad click — **this is the unique data surface vs all major Shopify-focused competitors**

### Source: Email / lead-form
- Pulled: form submissions, email opens/clicks (ESP-dependent)
- Computed: lead-to-sale conversion, time-to-close, multi-touch revenue allocation across email + ad

### Source: Organic / direct
- Pulled: referrer, UTM, landing page; "Source Links" generator
- Computed: organic-attributed revenue (with the "starts from install day" caveat — no historical backfill)

**Attribution models offered:** Last click, first click, linear, time-decay, "Scientific Mode" (proprietary hybrid), and custom-weighted models where the user splits credit "as they see fit."

The legacy "nested" report format is still accessible as an option.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Widget grid; widget types referenced for the iOS app (which mirrors the web app) include **Live Stream** (rolling list of incoming sales/leads), **Hyros Insights** (anomaly/opportunity callouts), **Recent Reports** (saved report shortcuts), and **Reporting Gap** (delta between Hyros-attributed sales and ad-platform-reported sales — likely the most distinctive widget). Drag-and-drop widget repositioning. Each widget has a configurable metric.
- **Interactions:** Drag-and-drop widget arrangement; per-widget custom-metric selection; date-range filter at top; click-through from any widget into deep reports.
- **Metrics shown:** Revenue, ROAS, CPA, leads, calls, sales — segmented by source/campaign/ad.
- **Source/screenshot:** No screenshot available. Reconstructed from https://apps.apple.com/us/app/hyros/id6450372574 and https://community.hyros.com/x/change-log/yuvugsgz2fw8/...

### Reports (Reporting page)
- **Path/location:** Sidebar > Reports.
- **Layout (prose):** Multi-pivot report builder. Filter/group by campaign, ad set, individual ad, landing page, traffic source, date range, attribution model, customer segment. Saved-report tabs at the top.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Drag-and-drop column configuration; attribution-model dropdown (Last click / First click / Linear / Time decay / Scientific Mode / Custom); per-row drill-into-lead-journey link.
- **Interactions:** Drill-down (row → lead journey), pivot (group toggle), saved-view tabs, export.
- **Metrics shown:** Spend, impressions, clicks, CTR, CPC, attributed revenue, ROAS, leads, calls booked, calls closed, AOV, LTV (cohort-bound), refund-adjusted ROAS.
- **Source/screenshot:** UI details not directly available — described in third-party reviews (smbguide.com, aazarshad.com).

### Lead Journey ("Deep Mode")
- **Path/location:** Reports row → click into a single customer; or Sidebar > Leads.
- **Layout (prose):** Full chronological touchpoint log for a single visitor — every page view, ad click, email open, click, form submit, call, and purchase event.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Vertical timeline; touchpoints rendered as rows with timestamp + source + event type; lead-journey "stages" assignment; parent-lead linking (handles cases where one user is multiple leads).
- **Interactions:** Stage-tagging, parent-lead reassignment, manual revenue assignment.
- **Source/screenshot:** Described in aazarshad.com and the iOS app feature list. UI details not visually observed.

### Call Tracking
- **Path/location:** Sidebar > Call Tracking.
- **Layout (prose):** DNI pool management; numbers replace the static phone number on the page per visiting session for ~1 hour. Inbound calls match back to the session and the originating ad click. Outcome tags (qualified / booked / sold) flow from the CRM via the Hyros API.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Number-pool inventory list; per-call detail view linking to the originating session and ad; outcome-tag editor.
- **Interactions:** Tag a call → re-feeds Meta/Google CAPI with the *outcome* (call quality), shifting bidding from call-volume to call-quality.
- **Metrics shown:** Calls booked, attended, qualified, sold; close rate per rep; revenue-per-call; attributed source.
- **Source/screenshot:** https://hyros.com/call-tracking + https://docs.hyros.com/setting-up-call-tracking/. No UI screenshots in marketing material.

### AIR (AI Remarketing)
- **Path/location:** Sidebar > AIR (separate paid module).
- **Layout (prose):** Visitor-recognition agent that fires personalized 1:1 emails/SMS based on Hyros tracking data. Uses Hyros's cross-customer network to identify returning visitors even without prior site contact.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Per-send revenue attribution; behavior-trigger configuration; message template editor.
- **Metrics shown:** Revenue per email (case study cites $6.54/email vs $3 baseline), 5%-of-attributed-revenue fee deducted on the same screen.

## Unique strengths

- **Call attribution end-to-end.** Twilio DNI pool + CRM outcome-tag round-trip (qualified/booked/sold) → fed back to Meta/Google CAPI as the bidding signal. No Shopify-focused competitor matches this — it's why coaching/info-product brands and high-ticket DTC pick Hyros.
- **Long-window multi-touch attribution.** Track from "first ad click to the 1000th"; attribute weeks- or months-long sales cycles. Triple Whale, Cometly, AdBeacon are all built for click-to-checkout windows.
- **Funnel-stack agnosticism.** Comfortable with mixed setups — landing page on ClickFunnels, checkout on ThriveCart, upsell in Stripe — instead of assuming Shopify checkout.
- **Deduped CAPI feed back to ad platforms.** The "AI Pixel Training" framing — feed cleaner conversions into Meta/Google's optimisers — is a core sell, and Hyros publishes the **29–33% more conversions captured** vs native ad-platform reporting figure that buyers cite.
- **AIR remarketing add-on.** Cross-customer visitor recognition network (visitors known to *any* Hyros customer can be re-identified on *your* site) — unusual data-graph asset that no Shopify-focused competitor advertises.
- **Subscription/LTV intelligence.** Tracks renewals, cancellations, upgrades, downgrades, refunds for years — relevant to subscription DTC and SaaS.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **Pricing opacity weaponised against buyers.** Demo gate before pricing is visible; published rate cards from third parties differ by 20–40% across sources, suggesting negotiated/floating pricing. Multiple Trustpilot complaints about **43% post-renewal price hikes**.
- **Aggressive sales tactics + refund denials.** Recurring Reddit and Trustpilot pattern: "90-day money-back guarantee" not honoured; users report being refused refunds even after weeks of broken setups.
- **Setup hell for SMB.** 2–12 weeks typical, 6+ months in worst cases reported. Multiple sources call out it needs a dedicated "owner who babysits it."
- **Steep learning curve / dense UI.** Hyros's own 2025–2026 announcement acknowledges this and is rolling out a "Basic View" specifically to address clutter complaints.
- **Tracked-revenue billing creates a perverse incentive.** Tier is set by attributed revenue, so improving attribution accuracy can auto-bump the customer to a higher tier — a recurring source of "surprise bill" complaints.
- **No historical import.** Tracking starts from install day; no retroactive backfill of orders/clicks (called out in the SegMetrics comparison).
- **Shopify-specific comparison losses.** Reviewers consistently say Hyros is overkill (and underbuilt) compared to Triple Whale, Cometly, or AdBeacon for a typical Shopify-only DTC store. Cometly and AdBeacon are cited as the alternatives users move to when they realise Hyros's call-tracking edge isn't relevant to their funnel.
- **Reporting latency.** Hands-on testing shows reports settling 10–20 min behind, vs. Cometly's near-minute updates.
- **No GA4 / no GSC integration.** Hyros positions as a *replacement* not a complement; multi-source-of-truth reconciliation is not in scope.
- **Polarised support.** Reviews split between "phenomenal" / "best in class" and "crappy customer service" / "one email a week" — likely tied to pricing tier and assigned analyst.
- **iOS app only at 4.0 stars / 8 ratings,** with the most recent surfaced review reporting crashes after updates.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **Different ICP, but a useful contrast.** Hyros's customer (high-ticket DTC, info products, call-based funnels) is exactly *not* Nexstage's Shopify/Woo SMB. Hyros is what those Nexstage-rejecting brands graduate to. Worth holding the line: do not bolt on call tracking trying to chase this segment — that's a different product.
- **The "Reporting Gap" widget is directly relevant to our 6-source thesis.** Hyros surfaces a delta between Hyros-attributed sales and ad-platform-reported sales as a top-level dashboard widget. That's a single-pair version of what our Real/Store/Facebook/Google/GSC/GA4 badges deliver across six lenses. Strong validation that "show the disagreement, don't pretend one number is truth" lands with paying users.
- **Pricing-by-tracked-revenue is a UX trap.** When attribution accuracy directly raises the customer's bill, every accuracy improvement has a cost-side downside for the customer. Our flat per-workspace pricing avoids this perverse incentive — worth flagging on positioning pages if we ever do a "vs Hyros" comparison.
- **Demo-gated pricing is the loudest single complaint.** Across G2, Trustpilot, Reddit, and review aggregators, "I had to book a call to see the price" is the #1 friction. Public, self-serve pricing is a real differentiator for SMB.
- **Cometly and AdBeacon are the named alternatives for SMB-leaning customers.** Multiple "Hyros alternative" articles explicitly recommend Cometly for Shopify-DTC and AdBeacon for performance-marketing teams citing simpler setup, cheaper, more accurate-feeling Shopify data. Worth reading their landing pages for cross-cut competitive language.
- **Shopify entry tier at $69/mo (up to $5K tracked) is a foothold play.** That's a deliberate land-and-expand move at the SMB segment — relevant if we see Hyros aggressively marketing into r/shopify.
- **Two view modes (Basic + Pro) is a recurring pattern across this batch.** Hyros's redesign mirrors what other competitors are converging on (simple default, expert mode opt-in). Worth a cross-cut decision if we don't already have one.
- **No GA4/GSC integration on Hyros's part is consistent with their "we are the source of truth" stance.** Nexstage's positioning is the opposite — multi-source reconciliation. Useful contrast in copy.
- **AIR's cross-customer visitor-recognition network is novel and probably privacy-fragile.** Worth tracking: GDPR/CCPA exposure on shared identification graphs. Could become a scandal trigger that shifts users elsewhere.
- **No historical import + day-1-only tracking** is a moat in reverse — once you're on Hyros, switching costs are high because you lose attribution history. Nexstage's historical-import job is worth keeping as a switching-cost weapon.
