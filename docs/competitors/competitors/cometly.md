## Positioning

Cometly is "marketing attribution software" that "transforms your marketing and sales data into insights, decisions, and scalable results by accurately attributing every conversion to its true source" (cometly.com). It targets three personas explicitly on the homepage: **B2B SaaS, ecommerce/DTC, and marketing agencies** — a wider net than Shopify-only attribution tools.

Their core differentiation pitch against Triple Whale is twofold: (1) "Cometly isn't just for ecommerce — we integrate with every major CRM, so you can track leads, pipeline, and revenue across any business model" (triple-whale-alternative page), and (2) an AI layer — "AI Chat built specifically for your ads data, giving you instant answers and actionable insights to optimize campaigns in real time." They market a proprietary "Gen-3 pixel tech" they claim delivers "20% higher data accuracy compared to competitors" (third-party tripleareview.com summary).

For ecommerce specifically the value prop is "Track every click, view, and purchase to see what drives sales — and scale the campaigns that actually work" (cometly.com/solutions/ecommerce). They lean heavily on the post-iOS-14 / cookie-deprecation story — server-side tracking + Conversion API enrichment as the technical wedge.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify
- **Pulled:** Order payment events via Shopify Webhooks (Settings > Notifications > Webhooks; event = `Order payment`, format = JSON). Cometly maps fields: First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone, `current_total_price` (amount), IP, plus the `comet_token` and `fingerprint` injected by their pixel into `theme.liquid`. Optionally Add to Cart and Initiate Checkout via separate webhook configs.
- **Computed:** Revenue attribution by ad campaign / audience / traffic source; AOV; LTV per customer ("Continuous LTV tracking per customer" — ads manager feature page); "checkout conversion data" and "leads & purchases by source" reports (cometly.com/solutions/ecommerce).
- **Attribution windows:** Default 28-day per aazarshad.com review of the Comet Pixel; the Attribution feature page mentions extended windows of "30, 60, and 90-day performance periods."

### Source: WooCommerce
- **Pulled:** Order events (presumed parity with Shopify based on the integrations directory listing and "Platform-Agnostic Tracking" claim). No Woo-specific docs surfaced in public sources.
- **Computed:** Same set as Shopify — revenue, AOV, LTV per customer.

### Source: Meta Ads
- **Pulled:** Campaign / ad-set / ad-level spend, impressions, clicks, conversions, ROAS — visible in the AI Ads Manager.
- **Computed:** Cometly-attributed revenue (separate from Meta-reported), CPA, ROAS by attribution model (First/Last/Linear/U-Shaped/Time Decay), Event Match Quality enhancement via server-side identity enrichment.
- **Push-back:** Meta Conversions API — "enriches each event with a depth of profile data" to lift Meta's EMQ score above Meta's "strong matching" threshold of 7.0.

### Source: Google Ads
- **Pulled:** Campaign / ad-group / ad performance.
- **Computed:** Same attribution models, conversion sync via Google's Enhanced Conversions / Conversions API.
- **Caveat:** A 2024-era tripleareview.com comparison stated Cometly "does offer some basic reporting and optimization features for Google Ads" but "advanced features like budget pacing, automated bidding, and creative testing are not available for Google Ads." Marketing copy in 2026 implies parity with Meta but the gap may persist.

### Source: TikTok Ads, LinkedIn Ads
- **Pulled:** Campaign-level spend + conversions via each platform's Conversions/Events API.
- **Computed:** Same multi-touch attribution treatment.

### Source: CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, etc.)
- **Pulled:** Lead → opportunity → closed-deal pipeline events. Used for "pipeline and revenue tracking" per the server-side tracking page.
- **Computed:** Revenue attribution down the funnel for B2B / SaaS use cases, "from first touch to closed deal."

### Source: Forms / Stripe / Webhooks
- **Pulled:** Lead-capture events (Typeform, Jotform, Webflow, etc.) and one-time/recurring payments (Stripe, Chargebee, Paddle).
- **Computed:** First-touch / last-touch / multi-touch attribution to source-of-traffic; LTV / continuous revenue tracking for subscription businesses.

### Sources NOT pulled (gap vs. Nexstage)
- **GA4** — not in integrations directory.
- **Google Search Console** — not in integrations directory.
- Cometly's "organic" tracking comes from their own pixel firing on the site, not from GSC. The marketing copy says "Real-time organic and paid ads attribution" — meaning organic-traffic referrer attribution, not GSC query data.

## Key UI patterns observed

### AI Ads Manager
- **Path/location:** Primary surface; sidebar nav. Replaces "switching between ad platforms."
- **Layout (prose):** Tabular ad-account view with rows for campaigns / ad sets / ads. Per the marketing page: "Customize Columns" lets users select which metrics to display; "Daily Breakdown" lets users view performance "day-by-day"; an attribution-model switcher lets users "switch between attribution models like First Touch, Last Touch, Linear, or U-Shaped" without leaving the table. An "Easily Switch Ad Accounts" control supports "single-click navigation between multiple accounts."
- **UI elements (concrete):** Multi-platform unified rows (Meta + Google + TikTok + LinkedIn in one table), customizable column picker, attribution-model dropdown, conversion-window selector, custom-metric builder using "existing data formulas," AI chat panel embedded in the same screen.
- **Interactions:** Drill-down from campaign → ad set → individual ad → creative-level performance. Bulk actions: "Manage budgets, pause under performers, and scale winners directly from Cometly without switching ad platforms" — i.e. the table is read-write (mutates the upstream ad platform via API). AI chat alongside surfaces "AI recommendations to optimize spend, creatives, and targeting."
- **Metrics shown:** Spend, impressions, clicks, conversions, revenue (Cometly-attributed), ROAS, CPA, custom metrics built from formulas. "Continuous LTV tracking per customer" is referenced.
- **Source:** https://www.cometly.com/features/ads-manager

### AI Chat
- **Path/location:** Conversational interface "embedded within the main Cometly application" (overlay or panel, exact placement not visible in public marketing).
- **Layout (prose):** ChatGPT-style query box. Marketing copy: "Ask AI anything about your ads, performance, or strategy — and get instant answers your whole team can use to stay aligned and take action."
- **UI elements (concrete):** Free-text input; structured response cards; example outputs include explicit budget recommendations like "Scale Adset 1 from $20/day to $50/day" with backing metrics (ROAS, CPA, conversion rate). Diagnostic queries supported: "What ads are working, what's not, and what to scale" and "ROAS drops to top performers."
- **Interactions:** Cross-platform queries: "ask one question and get cross-platform insights instantly" (one prompt synthesizes data across Meta + Google + TikTok + LinkedIn). Outputs are actionable suggestions, not just analysis.
- **Metrics shown:** Whatever the prompt asks for; AI has access to all connected ad-platform + conversion data.
- **Source:** https://www.cometly.com/features/ai-chat

### Multi-Touch Attribution / Customer Journey
- **Path/location:** Embedded in Ads Manager via attribution-model switcher; full journeys in a dedicated "Customer Journeys" / "Conversion Profiles" view.

## Unique strengths

- **AI Chat that mutates ad accounts.** Most analytics tools' AI is read-only ("explain this chart"). Cometly's AI Ads Manager pairs natural-language Q&A with write-back actions — "manage budgets, pause under performers, and scale winners directly from Cometly without switching ad platforms." The chat surfaces explicit dollar-level recommendations ("Scale Adset 1 from $20/day to $50/day") that the operator can execute in-app.
- **Server-side tracking is the default install path, not a paid upgrade.** The Cometly Pixel is "integrated from the start — no additional setup required," with the Conversion API enrichment story baked into core onboarding rather than offered as an add-on (e.g. how Triple Whale's "Triple Pixel" is positioned).
- **Cross-vertical (B2B + DTC).** Most ecommerce attribution tools (Triple Whale, Lifetimely, Polar) are Shopify-only or DTC-only. Cometly's CRM/forms/payments-heavy integration directory means a B2B SaaS lead-gen funnel and a Shopify DTC store can both run on the same platform — a genuine wedge against Triple Whale per their own marketing.
- **Event Match Quality lift narrative.** They publish content claiming server-side enrichment lifts Meta's EMQ score from sub-7 to 9+ — a measurable, vendor-defined number that's easier to reference in a sales conversation than "we improve attribution accuracy." Multiple Trustpilot reviewers cite specific EMQ score lifts (e.g. 4.5 → 9.4).
- **"AI Performance Reports" delivered to Slack/email.** Tagged as NEW on the features page; a low-friction async surface that competitors typically lack as a default.
- **Multi-workspace / agency-friendly.** Enterprise tier supports "multiple workspaces" with "workspace permissions" — agency users in testimonials (Aleric Heck/AdOutreach, Karlye Atwood/C Squared, Alvaro Berrios) cite this as a draw.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **Pricing opacity.** Public pricing page shows zero dollar amounts; Starter is $500/mo (high for SMBs). gethookd.ai, usermaven.com, and multiple Trustpilot reviewers cite "lack of pricing transparency" as a drawback. The legacy $199/mo Pro tier appears to no longer be the entry point.
- **Funnel-builder tracking gaps outside Shopify.** Repeated complaint that ClickFunnels and Funnelish tracking sits at ~50% match rate vs ~90% for Shopify, with purchase-value tracking failing on funnels — making CPA/ROAS unreliable for funnel-first businesses.
- **Google Ads support is shallower than Meta.** A 2024 tripleareview.com comparison flagged "advanced features like budget pacing, automated bidding, and creative testing are not available for Google Ads." aazarshad.com's earlier review went further: "Does not offer integration with Google Ads tracking" — though current marketing copy does claim Google Ads support, suggesting parity is improving but historically lagged Meta.
- **No GA4 / GSC integration.** Surprising for a tool that markets "real-time organic and paid ads attribution" — organic attribution is purely from their own pixel's referrer data, not Google's actual search-console queries.
- **Documentation quality and complex setups.** "Outdated documentation" and "complex setup, particularly with advanced systems" appear in multiple aggregator summaries. Several users describe support as slow / chat-only with 1 reply/day during issues.
- **Refund / cancellation friction.** A Sourceforge 1/5 review explicitly accuses the company of refusing cancellation; a Trustpilot 1-star cites "no guarantees or refund options" on a $5K commitment. 11% of Trustpilot reviews are 1-star (per WebSearch corpus snapshot).
- **No verified mobile app.** The kaleo.design case study mentions "Web and iOS" with mobile "in development" — no public app store listing observed in 2026.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **Cometly explicitly claims B2B SaaS as a target persona alongside DTC** — a position Triple Whale and Lifetimely don't take. If Nexstage's WorkspaceScope-based multi-tenancy ever pivots beyond Shopify/Woo (e.g. to bring in a HubSpot / Stripe revenue source for SaaS-ish merchants), Cometly is the closest precedent for that bridging.
- **AI Ads Manager is read-write, not read-only.** Their AI surfaces explicit dollar-level recommendations ("Scale Adset 1 from $20/day to $50/day") AND lets the operator execute them via API write-back (pause under-performers, change budgets) without leaving Cometly. This is a different bar than "ask AI to explain a chart" — relevant if Nexstage ever extends its `MetricSourceResolver` thinking to AI-generated suggestions backed by live data.
- **Server-side pixel + Conversion API is treated as the default install, not an upsell.** Their onboarding wizard front-loads `comet_token` injection into Shopify's `theme.liquid` and webhook configuration. Compare with Nexstage's import-via-API path that does not require pixel install — Cometly's wedge is "we capture sessions native trackers miss," not "we aggregate what the platform already gives you."
