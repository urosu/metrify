## Positioning

StoreHero pitches itself as a "profit-first" analytics platform for scaling DTC brands and the agencies that serve them, explicitly replacing what they call "attribution-only reporting" (their framing for Triple Whale) and "self-serve profit trackers" (their framing for BeProfit and Lifetimely). Their North-Star metric is contribution margin — net sales minus COGS minus marketing spend — and almost every screen, blog post, and academy lesson is anchored to that one number. The company is Dublin-based, founded 2022 by Conor McElligott and Ciarán McKenna (ex-Wolfgang Digital), raised €600k pre-seed in July 2023, listed at $1.2M revenue with 11 staff in 2025; the Shopify app launched 16 May 2025 and has 13 reviews (all 5-star) as of April 2026. Founder-led narrative is loud about "profit, not revenue", and they leverage a small but visible Irish/UK DTC customer roster (Fíor Jewellery, Origin Coffee, Kinvara Skincare, Nomad the Label, Gigi Supplements) for credibility.

## Product surfaces (their app's information architecture)

Pulled from the storehero.ai/features page, the Academy "Platform Deep Dive" course, the Shopify App Store screenshots list, and the agency landing page. The primary tab strip in-app is: **Dashboard, Ads, Creatives, Finance, LTV, Products, Orders**. Sub-screens and modules:

- **Dashboard (Unified)** — single-screen funnel from reach to profit; top-level KPI tiles for sales, ad spend, contribution margin
- **Spend Advisor** — "what does the next $100 of ad spend do to profit" simulator with pause / pivot / scale recommendations
- **Ads** — channel-by-channel paid performance (Meta, Google, TikTok); blended view available
- **Creative Overview / Creatives** — per-creative performance for Meta ad images, video, copy
- **Finance / P&L** — full P&L split by day, week, or month on a single screen
- **LTV (Lifetime Value Overview)** — customer lifetime value summary
- **Cohorts** — LTV cohort tables (separate sub-page or tab inside LTV)
- **Products** — fully-loaded COGS, gross profit per SKU, breakeven ROAS per SKU
- **Orders** — order-level margin monitoring
- **Store Overview** — high-level store performance summary
- **Markets** — country/region-level profitability split
- **Goals & Forecasting** — annual goal entry, auto-generated seasonally-adjusted monthly benchmarks, traffic-light deviation flags
- **Cost Settings** — configuration for product costs, shipping, fulfillment/packaging, transaction fees, marketing
- **Email Reports** — daily/weekly/monthly digest builder (delivered to email + Slack)
- **iOS Mobile App** — read-only summary of sales & profit reports
- **StoreHero Academy** — separate sub-domain (academy.storehero.ai) for courses + certification, not in-app
- **Agency Multi-Store Dashboard** — white-labelled, aggregated view across multiple client stores

That's ~17 distinct surfaces in the main app, plus Academy and the Agency aggregator. T1-typical inventory.

## Unique strengths

- **Contribution margin as the single anchor metric across every screen** — every tab, every report, every academy lesson is hung off contribution margin (net sales − COGS − marketing spend). No other competitor in this batch is this monomaniacal about a single profit metric.
- **Spend Advisor "live next-$100" simulator** — turns ad-spend planning into an interactive what-if; competitors mostly show historical ROAS, not forward simulation tied to profit.
- **Built-in seasonal forecasting from a single annual goal input** — auto-generates month-by-month benchmarks with a green/red traffic-light deviation flag. Most "profit dashboards" require manual monthly target entry.
- **Hands-on onboarding & DTC-specialist support as a paid SKU** (Elite Support tier) — productizes consultative help that competitors offer ad-hoc.
- **Native MCP / Claude integration** — listed as a top-level nav item; almost no competitor in the analytics-for-DTC space has surfaced this.
- **Dedicated agency multi-client dashboard with white-labelling** — unlike Lifetimely / BeProfit which are single-store-first.
- **Cost-Settings module covers all five cost buckets explicitly** (product costs, shipping, fulfillment/packaging, transaction fees, marketing) — disciplined cost taxonomy.
- **All 13 Shopify App Store reviews are 5-star** — small sample but a strong NPS signal vs. broader-listed competitors with mixed ratings.

- **Pinterest Ads not supported** — flagged in the only public negative reviewer commentary.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **Contribution-margin-first is their wedge against Triple Whale's attribution-first.** They treat it as a category split: "marketing performance tool, not a business performance tool" is their explicit framing of Triple Whale. Useful framing reference for Nexstage positioning if we want to plant a flag elsewhere on that 2x2.
- **Cost-config taxonomy maps cleanly to ours** — five cost buckets (product, shipping, fulfillment/packaging, transaction fees, marketing) and a dedicated Cost Settings screen. Reasonable to assume their UpdateCostConfig flow triggers a recompute, though the mechanism isn't public — worth comparing to our retroactive `RecomputeAttributionJob` UX.
- **Spend Advisor "live next-$100 → profit" simulator is a notable interaction primitive.** It's neither a chart nor a table — it's an input → live calculation → discrete recommendation pill. Direct analog opportunity for our Real / Store / Facebook / Google source-badge thesis when applied to forward decisions.
- **Goals module uses two-state (green/red) traffic light, not three-state.** They explicitly describe it as "green & red" with no amber/yellow middle. Worth noting given UX battles around traffic-light semantics.
- **Pricing is revenue-bracket scaling, not per-store / per-seat / per-event.** One axis. Simple to communicate to buyers, harder to reconcile with multi-brand agency rollouts (which is partly why the agency page goes "Book a Demo").
- **MCP-for-Claude integration is in their top-level nav.** No technical detail public, but the marketing placement suggests they see it as a meaningful differentiator; signals AI-agent surface is becoming table-stakes for analytics tools.
- **App Store has a Free tier (Ad/Web/SEO reports only) that the main pricing page does not surface.** Land-and-expand wedge — probably how they get into the funnel before revenue-bracket pricing kicks in.
- **The marketing-page-only customer roster (Fíor Jewellery, Origin Coffee, Kinvara Skincare, Nomad the Label, Gigi Supplements, Madcraft Agency, Bearhouse, VIBAe) is heavily Irish/UK.** Low US footprint visible. Geographic positioning gap.
- **Pinterest Ads being the lone missing-channel complaint is interesting for batch 2 patterns** — across competitors in this category, Pinterest tends to be deprioritized but is loud when it's missing.
- **They pair "fix tracking, then add fancy attribution on top" rhetoric with no published attribution-window or methodology.** Pressure point for Nexstage's source-badge transparency thesis: showing the buyer "this number came from THIS source on THIS window" is a legible counter to the StoreHero-style "trust the contribution margin" black box.
- **Academy + 1:1 onboarding + Slack/email digests are bundled into base pricing** — the human-touch concierge experience is part of the product, not an add-on (Elite Support adds more, but the base already includes guided onboarding).
- **No public dashboard-customisation surface observed.** Reports appear to be opinionated/fixed rather than user-configurable. Could be either a strength (less decision fatigue) or weakness (ceiling for power users); BeProfit calls it out as a gap competitively.
