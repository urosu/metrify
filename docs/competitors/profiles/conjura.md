## Positioning

Conjura sells "AI Powered eCommerce Analytics That Drive Profit" — the homepage tagline frames every dashboard around contribution profit rather than revenue/ROAS. Their explicit anti-positioning is Triple Whale ("Where Triple Whale leans on pixels, Conjura digs deep into first-party data, tracking true SKU-level contribution profit") and Metorik ("designed to unify store data, marketing data, and customer behavior into a single profit-led view"). The 2026 narrative angle is **Owly AI**, a natural-language analytics agent marketed as "ChatGPT but for your entire eCommerce performance data." They target omnichannel brands (DTC + Amazon + marketplace + wholesale) explicitly — a clear differentiator from Shopify-only tools.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify / BigCommerce / WooCommerce / Magento

- **Pulled:** orders, line items, customers, products/variants, refunds, revenue, discounts, tax data, stock levels.
- **Computed:** contribution profit (revenue minus COGS, shipping, fees, refunds, ad spend), contribution margin, AOV, repurchase rate, LTV, sell-through rate, refund rate, discount %.
- **Attribution windows:** Not explicitly published. LTV calculations use a fixed 12-month window from first order ("the average value of the orders placed by the customers in your selected cohort within 12 months").

### Source: Meta Ads / Google Ads / TikTok Ads / Bing Ads / Pinterest Ads

- **Pulled:** ad spend, impressions, clicks, platform-attributed conversions, platform-attributed revenue (per Campaign Deepdive KPI definitions).
- **Computed:** CPM (`spend/(1000×impressions)`), CTR (`clicks/impressions`), CPC (`spend/clicks`), Last Click ROAS (`last click revenue/spend`), Platform ROAS (`platform revenue/spend`), CAC, customers acquired.
- **Attribution windows:** Two models exposed in the UI side-by-side: **Last Click** (Conjura's own session-based attribution) and **Platform Attributed** (passes through what Meta/Google/TikTok report via their own models). Lookback window not published.
- **SKU-level ad spend attribution:** Done via the URL of the ad. Works for Google Shopping, Performance Max, deep-linked Google ads, and Facebook ads where the URL points to a product page. Falls into "Ad Spend - No Product" bucket when the ad lands on a generic homepage. **No attribution model or lookback window published for this.**

### Source: Amazon (Seller Partner + Amazon Ads)

- **Pulled:** Amazon orders, ASIN-level performance, Amazon ad spend.
- **Computed:** Amazon-specific contribution profit per SKU, marketplace stock, sell-through rate.
- Heavily emphasized in 2026 marketing copy — they call themselves "the #1 Amazon analytics tool in 2026 for brands that care about profit, not vanity metrics."

### Source: GA4

- **Pulled:** sessions, channel/source attribution, web analytics.
- **Computed:** Last-click conversion rate (`last click conversions/sessions`), session-attributed revenue.
- Described as the source for "channel and session information" feeding Performance Trends.

### Source: ERP / OMS (Linnworks, Brightpearl, Cin7, ShipStation, NetSuite)

- **Pulled:** unified inventory, fulfilment costs, multi-warehouse stock.
- **Computed:** Feeds into stock-level metrics, sell-through, restock forecasts. Gated behind Scale tier.

### Source: TikTok Shop / eBay / Walmart

- **Pulled:** marketplace orders, marketplace ad spend (where applicable).
- **Computed:** Cross-marketplace omnichannel profit roll-up.

### New vs Existing Customers Dashboard

- **Path/location:** Customer Analytics section.
- **Layout (prose):** Side-by-side comparison view of first-time vs returning customers, with daily snapshots. "AOV, discount %, and profit margin differ across both groups."
- **UI elements (concrete):** Two-panel layout (new vs existing) for behavioral metrics. Channel performance breakdown showing "which channels are best for acquiring new customers" vs repeat purchases. Product-level split: "products that attract new customers vs the ones your loyal base can't resist."
- **Interactions:** Daily snapshots; comparison filters by product, channel, discount.
- **Metrics shown:** AOV (new vs existing), discount % (new vs existing), profit margin (new vs existing), order share by channel, top products per segment.
- **Source:** https://www.conjura.com/new-vs-existing-customers-dashboard

### LTV Analysis Dashboard

- **Path/location:** Customer Analytics section.
- **Layout (prose):** **Heatmap visualization.** Y-axis (vertical): customer cohorts by acquisition month. X-axis (horizontal): months since first order. Each cell shows average customer LTV at that intersection (example given: "the average value of each customer acquired in May 2024 after 2 months" = €405.28). Color intensity encodes magnitude: "The darker the colour, the higher the lifetime value."
- **UI elements (concrete):** Help-doc explicitly describes three pattern types users should look for: **horizontal patterns** (cohort-level effects, certain acquisition groups outperform), **vertical patterns** (lifecycle stages where LTV accelerates, e.g. month 3 post-acquisition), **diagonal patterns** (calendar effects, e.g. all cohorts had an LTV lift in October 2024). Anomalies appear as outlier cells.
- **Interactions:** Filter/segment by acquisition channel, brand, SKU, or region.
- **Metrics shown:** Customers Acquired (cohort size), CAC (`spend/customers acquired`), Order Frequency (`orders within 12mo / cohort size`), AOV (`revenue 12mo / orders 12mo`), Repeat Rate (`repeat purchasers / cohort size`), LTV Gross Revenue (`revenue 12mo / cohort size`), LTV Gross Profit (`(revenue - COGS) 12mo / cohort size`), LTV:CAC ratio.
- **Source:** https://www.conjura.com/ltv-analysis-dashboard, https://help.conjura.com/en/articles/9965367-tips-how-to-read-and-interpret-the-ltv-heatmap, https://help.conjura.com/en/articles/8867161-kpi-definitions-ltv-analysis

### Purchase Patterns Dashboard

- **Path/location:** Customer Analytics section.
- **Layout (prose):** **5-column table.** Each column = customer's Nth order (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th). Each column contains stacked **cards** representing products, categories, or brands depending on the granularity setting. Top of column 1 always shows "100% of Unique Customers" since all customers must place a 1st order. Each card shows what % of customers at that stage purchased that item (verbatim example: "62.5% of new customers"). Cards display unique customer count, gross revenue, and order contribution %.
- **UI elements (concrete):** Card-based grid. Sort metric controls (unique customers / gross revenue / order contribution). Granularity selector (product / category / brand).
- **Interactions:** **Click any card to filter the entire dashboard** to only customers who purchased that item at that stage. Reveals: (a) what was bought in the same order, (b) what those customers bought in subsequent orders, (c) progressive funnel narrowing across columns.
- **Metrics shown:** % of customers purchasing each item per order-rank, unique customers, gross revenue, order contribution %.
- **Source:** https://www.conjura.com/purchase-patterns-dashboard, https://help.conjura.com/en/articles/10222244-tips-how-to-read-and-interpret-the-purchase-patterns-dashboard

## Unique strengths

- **SKU-level ad-spend attribution via ad URL.** They attribute ad spend down to SKU by parsing the ad's destination URL — works for Google Shopping, Performance Max, deep-linked Google ads, and Facebook ads. Anything pointing to a generic homepage falls into a "Ad Spend - No Product" bucket. This is a published methodology — most competitors don't disclose theirs.
- **Side-by-side dual attribution (Last Click vs Platform Attributed).** The Campaign Deepdive shows both Conjura's session-based last-click columns and the platforms' own attributed columns simultaneously. Direct analog to a multi-source-of-truth UI pattern.
- **Cohort heatmap with interpretation guidance.** Their help docs explicitly teach users to read horizontal/vertical/diagonal patterns in the LTV heatmap — pedagogical onboarding most competitors skip.
- **Purchase Patterns 5-column funnel.** A unique IA pattern: 5 columns for 1st through 5th orders, click-to-filter funnel discovery. No other competitor in this space publishes anything similar.
- **Omnichannel from day one.** Native Amazon Seller Partner + eBay + Walmart + TikTok Shop in addition to Shopify/BigCommerce/Woo/Magento. Most analytics tools are Shopify-first.
- **Profit-first metric framing.** Every dashboard headline is "Contribution Profit" not "Revenue" or "ROAS." Their stack of derived metrics — contribution profit per SKU, LTV Gross Profit, LTV:CAC (gross profit) — is consistently profit-led.
- **ERP/OMS integrations on premium tier** (Linnworks, Brightpearl, Cin7, NetSuite) — rare for an analytics SaaS targeting SMB.
- **Owly AI as a separately-priced product.** Treats natural-language analytics as a distinct add-on at $199+/mo with hard quotas (250 queries / 50 reports per tier) — explicit monetization of LLM costs.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **No GSC / no Klaviyo.** Two major sources missing for an SMB ecommerce analytics tool — SEO/search data and email/SMS attribution.
- **No first-party server-side pixel.** Triple Whale's Triple Pixel is a deliberate non-feature for Conjura — they claim it's a strength ("first-party data" framing) but it's a real gap for merchants in iOS-loss/post-tracking-collapse contexts.
- **Pricing is opaque and inconsistent.** Three different price surfaces (website $19.99–$129.99 / Shopify App Store $299–$499 / comparison page $799–$899) with no visible reconciliation. Real prices are gated behind a sales call.
- **Custom data connector limitation** mentioned in one Capterra review: brands with niche data sources need custom-built connectors that take longer.
- **Daily refresh, not real-time** for the underlying data layer ("refreshed nightly" per Capterra review and "updated daily" on Performance Overview page). Owly AI marketing implies real-time but this is not substantiated.
- **No mobile app.** No iOS or Android app surfaced in searches — web-only.
- **Owly AI UI surface is undisclosed.** Marketing relies on positioning ("ChatGPT for your data") without showing how questions are typed, where the chat lives in the UI, or how multi-turn conversations work. The "Owly AI Short.gif" is referenced but not embedded for review on public pages.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **6 source badges thesis check:** Conjura exposes only 2 attribution columns (Last Click and Platform Attributed) per ad-platform metric, not 6 simultaneous source lenses. Their UI pattern of side-by-side attribution columns is closer than Triple Whale's pixel-only view, but narrower than the Real/Store/Facebook/Google/GSC/GA4 set Nexstage proposes.
- **No GSC integration** — clear differentiator for Nexstage. Conjura indexes GA4 sessions but does not pull search-query data. Worth noting on the SEO surface specifically.
