## Positioning

BeProfit (operated by Become / now branded under "Viably" per the help-center subdomain `help.runviably.com`) markets itself as "The Most Powerful Profit Analysis & Optimization Solution" and "the leading unified dashboard that turns multi-store complexity into clear confident decisions" (homepage). It targets multichannel SMB merchants who want net-profit visibility (not just revenue/ROAS) across Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon, and Wix in a single dashboard. Headline outcome claims on the homepage are "+20% Contribution Profit Within 3 Months", "+22% Profit on Ad Spend Within 3 Months", and "5-10 Weekly Hours Analysis Time Saved." The product replaces spreadsheet-based P&L tracking and competes head-to-head with Lifetimely, TrueProfit, and Sumtracker for the lower end of the profit-tracker category.

## Pricing & tiers

- A search result references a separate "Plus Plan at $450 a month" tier; not corroborated on the current Shopify listing — possibly a historical SKU.

## Data they expose

### Source: Shopify (and WooCommerce/Wix/Amazon — same model)
- Pulled: orders, line items, products, vendors, customers, refunds, fulfillments, taxes, discounts, processing fees, country, sales channel.
- Computed: gross profit, net profit, contribution profit, AOV, repurchase rate, LTV, CAC, ROAS, POAS (Profit on Ad Spend), per-order/per-product/per-country/per-shop/per-platform profit margins, returns rate.
- Cohort granularity: weekly, monthly, yearly (cohort analysis docs).

### Source: Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
- Pulled: campaign/adset/ad spend, impressions, clicks, conversions.
- Computed: ROAS, POAS, CPC, conversion rate, CAC, attributed revenue (last-click via UTM with first-click fallback rules).
- Attribution: UTM-based; pixel guidance referenced in their blog but BeProfit does not appear to operate its own server-side pixel.

### Source: Google Ads
- Pulled: campaign-level spend, clicks, impressions.
- Computed: ROAS, CPC, CPA.
- Attribution rule (verbatim from help center): "for Google specifically: we attribute based on UTM data only to avoid attributing SEO-based orders to the Google Ads platform." This is the source of a recurring complaint — see "What users hate."

### Source: TikTok / Pinterest / Bing / Snapchat Ads
- Pulled: ad spend (campaign-level).
- Computed: ROAS, POAS — unified into the Marketing reports dashboard.

### Source: Klaviyo
- Listed as integration; specific pulled fields not documented in public pages.

### Source: CJ Dropshipping
- Pulled: per-product COGS auto-synced.

### Attribution model (verbatim from help.runviably.com)

> "BeProfit uses last-click attribution" with two exceptions:
>
> 1. "If an order's last click is direct and first click is not direct, we attribute the order to the first click."
> 2. "If an order's last click is from the same source as the first click AND the last click has no UTM data, we attribute the order to the first click."

Manual override path: Data Settings > UTM Attribution → scroll the UTM List → assign each UTM group to a traffic source (or "Other" for SMS/Social/Email) → Save.

### GA4 / GSC
Not observed in any source. BeProfit does not advertise GA4 or GSC connections.

## Unique strengths

- **POAS (Profit on Ad Spend) as a first-class headline metric** — appears alongside ROAS in the dashboard hero row; not just buried in a marketing report. This is more aggressive profit-positioning than most competitors who lead with ROAS.
- **LTV/CAC payback "green triangle" overlay** in the cohort grid — single-glance visual of when each cohort crosses the CAC line. Distinctive viz; not seen on Lifetimely/TrueProfit.
- **Multi-platform reach in one tier:** Shopify + WooCommerce + Wix + Amazon under one BeProfit account (Plus tier) — broader platform coverage than Lifetimely (Shopify-only) and most competitors.
- **Conditional / variable expense engine** — can set per-order expenses as percentage-of-revenue or fixed amount, with conditions on order status / sales channel / items count. More flexible than spreadsheet-style flat COGS.
- **Cohort calculation flexibility** — "Average Per Customer" vs "Total Cohort" toggle, and base-metric switching between ROAS / gross profit / repurchase rate. Most cohort tools fix one of these axes.
- **Shopify Staff Pick designation** (referenced in multiple third-party reviews) — credibility and discoverability boost in the Shopify ecosystem.

## Unique weaknesses / common complaints

- **Google Ads spend under-counting.** Highest-impact complaint pattern: BeProfit attributes Google Ads spend only via UTM-matched traffic, ignoring un-UTM'd or SEO-conflated spend. Users report only ~15% of real Google Ads spend gets imported, "massively inflating profit figures." This stems from the documented attribution rule: "for Google specifically: we attribute based on UTM data only to avoid attributing SEO-based orders to the Google Ads platform" (help center, verbatim).
- **Subscription / billing / cancellation friction.** Multiple 2025-2026 1-star reviews cite charges continuing months after cancellation requests; one reviewer reports $720/year billed for an unused install with no response from support.
- **Support latency.** "Support took days to answer with lazy, unhelpful responses" / "Staff super slow to respond" — recurring across multiple negative reviews in 2024-2026. Contradicts the positive cohort that praises "Anthony" specifically.
- **No mobile app.** Confirmed by competitor comparison page; BeProfit's own marketing does not advertise mobile.
- **No GA4 / no GSC integration.** Pure paid-channel + on-site data; merchants relying on organic-traffic analytics need a separate tool.
- **Multi-store gated to top tier.** Anything beyond a single shop forces the $249/mo Plus plan — a steep cliff vs Lifetimely's per-shop pricing.
- **Order-volume ceilings drive forced upgrades.** 450 → 900 → 1,700 → unlimited; merchants growing past each ceiling face automatic tier jumps.
- **"Calculation Preferences" stability bugs** reported in early-2026 reviews — at least one user reports it not working at all, breaking profit calculations.
- **Initial setup data-entry burden** — multiple reviewers note product-cost / expense entry is heavy upfront; not a "5-minute install" tool.

## Notes for Nexstage

- **The Google Ads attribution complaint is a direct opportunity.** BeProfit only counts Google Ads spend that maps via UTMs; merchants flagging this in 1-star reviews want full ad-platform spend ingested even when un-UTM'd. Nexstage's per-source data model (Real / Google / GA4 etc. badges) should explicitly reconcile platform-reported spend vs UTM-attributed revenue rather than dropping un-mapped spend. Worth flagging in attribution / cost-config feature research.
- **POAS as a hero metric.** BeProfit elevates "Profit on Ad Spend" to dashboard-headline status. Nexstage's KPI deck and `MetricSourceResolver` should consider whether POAS is a derived metric we expose by default (computed: net profit / ad spend, both already available per-workspace).
- **Cost configuration UX is form-heavy and gated.** The `Settings > Costs > {Fulfillment, Processing Fees, Calculation Preferences, Marketing Platforms}` split plus `Custom Operational Expenses` / `Products Costs` is a 5-section settings tree. Multiple reviewers cite setup burden. Direct analog for our cost-config area — consider whether we collapse/streamline.
- **Multi-store comparison is monetized hard ($249/mo).** This is BeProfit's #1 paywall; their pitch is "compare shops in one place." For multi-tenant Nexstage workspaces, if we expose cross-workspace comparison cheaply we have a clear pricing-disruption story.
- **The "green triangle on cohort row = CAC payback crossover"** is a strong UI primitive worth studying for our cohort screens — single-glance, no explanation needed.
- **UTM Attribution is the only attribution model.** No pixel, no server-side, no platform-direct attribution side-by-side. Nexstage's 6-source-badge thesis (Real / Store / Facebook / Google / GSC / GA4) is structurally differentiated — BeProfit users have to manually reconcile UTM gaps.
- **No GA4, no GSC.** This is the cleanest gap vs Nexstage's stack; SEO-driven brands cannot use BeProfit alone.
- **No mobile app.** A consistent gap — multiple competitors in this profile batch (TrueProfit) lead with mobile. Worth tracking whether SMB profit-tracker buyers actively want this.
- **"Predicted Cost" feature** referenced in FAQ — calculates a return estimation curve over multiple days (e.g., "10% of order value first day, 7% second day"). Suggests they model returns as a decaying function rather than waiting for actual returns to land. Interesting cost-config primitive.
- **Branding ambiguity:** the help center is now hosted at `help.runviably.com/beprofit/`, suggesting BeProfit is being absorbed into a parent brand "Viably" — worth tracking for product-direction signals over the next 6-12 months.
- **Capterra has only 4 reviews; Shopify App Store has 188.** Real user-feedback volume is concentrated in the Shopify App Store reviews — that's the primary signal source for any future research on this competitor.
- **Screenshot acquisition blocker:** No clean public PNGs of the dashboards beyond the 9-image Shopify App Store carousel. Help center articles are gated behind 404s on the new `runviably.com` domain, and the marketing pages use stylized illustrations rather than real product screenshots. UI specifics for the customizable dashboard, profit simulators, and profit insights screens remain unobserved.
